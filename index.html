Dining & Entertainment

In addition to great fishing, water sports and incredible views, you can explore St. George Island’s many other attractions or head into town for family-friendly restaurants and great entertainment. Visit the sugar white sand beach at the State Park for some of the best shelling around or learn about the Island’s native and seasonal wildlife at the Apalachicola National Estuarine Research Reserve. You can also explore the Historic St. George Island Lighthouse and Museum, and kids of all ages will enjoy the Lighthouse playground. Kids can also visit the game room at BJ’s Pizza & Subs or delight in the aisles of treats at Sugar Kingdom. Most of the restaurants like The Blue Parrot, Harry A’s, Paddy’s, the Beach Pit, and Mango Mike’s offer a children’s menu, as well as a great selection of oysters and other fresh seafood, BBQ, steaks, chicken and more. After dinner, head over to Aunt Ebby’s for delicious ice cream or stay in town for Bingo, Trivia Night or great live entertainment from musicians like John Sutton, Ashley Morgan, Shell Shock, the Frank Fletcher Duo and other talented regional entertainers. You can also go over the bridge to Eastpoint for more great dining at places like the Eastpoint Beer Company, Mangia 850, El Jalisco, the Red Pirate Family Grill & Mini Golf and many more. There are even more great restaurants and entertainment options in Apalachicola. When it comes to dining and entertainment, St. George Island the Forgotten Coast have you covered.

A View from the Sky

St. George Island Florida is a beautiful 22-mile barrier island in the Gulf of Mexico and is home to some of the most stunning views of the Gulf in all of the Sunshine State. Uncrowded beaches are perfect for family-friendly activities—from swimming, fishing, kayaking and paddleboarding in the clear gulf waters to shelling, castle-building and relaxing on the sugar white sand. Nature lovers can venture over to the Apalachicola Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve or out to St. George Island’s beautiful bay marshes to enjoy extraordinary wildlife and stunning views of the sunset. The Plantation on St. George Island, where this beautiful home is located, is a coastal gated community with five miles of pristine, white-sand beaches, the famous Apalachicola Bay, towering pines, and a variety of incredible birds like Peregrine Falcons, Bald Eagles, Osprey, and Great Blue Heron, just to name a few. After a long day of fun and adventure, you can relax and gaze with awe at the star-studded southern sky where the Milky Way is clearly visible.

Birdwatching

St. George Island has some of the best bird watching in Florida. The State Park, beautiful marshlands and untouched coastline are home to more than 300 species of birds, as well as seasonal species that visit in the spring and fall like Scissor-tailed Flycatchers and Western Kingbirds. Bird watchers may be lucky enough to see Peregrine Falcons, Bald Eagles, Osprey, Great Blue Heron, Egrets, Orchard Orioles, Prairie Warblers, Snowy Plover, Indigo Buntings, Bonaparte’s Gulls, Royal Tern, and Red-cockaded Woodpeckers, Scarlet Tanagers and Owls just to name a few.
